# Disclaimer & Responsible Use

Yosoi exists as research into API design, web reverse engineering, and, honestly, general fun. It's a tool for understanding how the web is built and how it talks to itself. Treat it that way.

## Respect the people on the other end

**Sites that don't want to be hit shouldn't be hit.** If a site asks you not to scrape it through its `robots.txt`, its terms of service, a rate limit, a block, or just plain common sense; consider listening and not being a dummy. There's a real person and real infrastructure on the other side of every request. Don't be the reason their pager goes off.

**Please don't route around IP bans with Tor or a VPN.** Those are public commodities; shared exits that other people depend on for privacy and access. They are not meant to be burned through for bulk scraping, and abusing them gets the exits blocked for everyone. If you've been banned, take the hint instead of laundering your traffic through infrastructure that isn't yours.

## You own the risk

**You, the person running this, assume all legal and ethical risk. Not the tool, not the code, not the authors, not Cascading Labs.** Yosoi is provided "as is," without warranty of any kind, for research and educational purposes. How you use it, against whom, and under what jurisdiction's laws is entirely on you. Know the laws that apply to you (computer-misuse statutes, terms of service, copyright, data-protection rules like GDPR/CCPA) and stay on the right side of them.

## If you go gray or black hat anyway

We're not naive about what tools like this get used for. But if you choose to color outside the lines: **for the love of god, be respectful about it.** Throttle yourself. Don't take sites down. Don't hoard data you have no right to. And don't do anything so reckless or so loud that it gets this project, and the open research it enables for everyone else shut down.

Be the kind of person the rest of us can point to when someone asks whether this tool should exist.
